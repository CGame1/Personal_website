Chloe Game

Postdoctoral Fellow, MSCA SEAS Fellow — Department of Informatics, University of Bergen

Marine scientist working at the nexus of benthic ecology, image processing, computer vision and machine learning. I investigate how computer science and technology can best support exploration and understanding of the marine environment, with emphasis on accessibility, standardisation and real-world impact.

Short profile

I hold a PhD in Computer Science (University of East Anglia) focused on extracting and processing ecological data from seabed images. My work develops machine learning and image‑processing methods (including novel underwater image enhancement) to make automated analysis accessible to ecologists without specialist ML training.

Research summary

My research began in benthic ecology studying environmental drivers of species distributions and has evolved into applied informatics research. Current work combines seabed imagery and environmental sensor data to improve automated monitoring of vulnerable marine ecosystems, emphasising explainability, generalisability and operational monitoring workflows.

Selected projects

Developing automated multi-modal monitoring strategies of vulnerable marine ecosystems (VMEs) (2024–2027). Part of the SEAS programme (Shaping European Research Leaders for Marine Sustainability), funded by Marie Skłodowska‑Curie grant agreement No. 101034309.

Research Interests

My research interests generally include:

  • Benthic ecology & biodiversity monitoring
  • Machine learning for environmental monitoring
  • Image processing and underwater image enhancement
  • Explainable and generalisable automated analysis workflows

Project focus

The current project develops ML methods that combine seabed images with environmental covariates (e.g., topography, temperature) to improve classification accuracy and ecological relevance. Emphasis is placed on methods that scale to large monitoring programs and provide interpretable outputs for stakeholders.
